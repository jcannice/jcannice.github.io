Spam/Ham Classifier

Let's sort out the junk, shall we?

Overview

In my Techniques of Data Science class, I was tasked with building a classifier capable of distinguishing spam emails from ham (non-spam) emails. My model performed with a 90% accuracy on a hidden set of emails.

EDA

To begin identifying distinguishing features, I explored and compared differences between ham and spam emails in the labeled data set.

This barplot demonstrates the difference in usage of words and tags that I believed to be indicative of spam. The html tag in particular appears much more often in junk mail due to the common inclusion of illicit/malignant links.


This class conditional density plot shows how ham emails are more often shorter in length than their spam counterparts.


Feature Engineering

I extracted more features primarily from the text body of the emails.

  • Percentage of exclamation marks in body.
  • Percentage of capital letters in body.
  • Word count in subject line.
  • Word count in body.

Model Fitting & Performance

I fit the training data to a logistic regression model given the binary classification goal. The resulting accuracy when performing on the test set was 88.9% and 90.3% when tested on the hidden data.
